# Tooltip

Tooltips provide helpful, additional content for their paired elements on hover. They are contextual and specific to the element and don't provide the big picture.

## Content

Tooltips with obvious or redundant text are not beneficial to users. If you can’t think of particularly helpful content, don’t offer a tooltip. Otherwise, you’ll just add information pollution to your UI and waste the time of users.

<Use>
	<Use.Do>
		<ul>
			<li>Use tooltips consistently throughout your site.</li>
			<li>Try to make the text as short as possible and include the most critical or meaningful info to make reading easier.</li>
			<li>If the tooltip has more than one sentence, use a period at the end.</li>
		</ul>
  </Use.Do>
	<Use.Dont>
		<ul>
			<li>Don't use tooltips for simple actions users perform regularly.</li>
			<li>Avoid tooltips which are complete duplicates of what is stated clearly in the UI.</li>
			<li>Don't use tooltips for information that is necessary to task completion, e.g. field or password requirements. Such info essential to a frictionless user experience should always be present on the screen.</li>
			<li>If the tooltip is one phrase, don't use a period at the end even if it's a complete sentence in order not to overload the interface.</li>
		</ul>
  </Use.Dont>
</Use>
